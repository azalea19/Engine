7 /**
8 * @file FrameBuffer.h
9 * @Author Maddisen Topaz
10 * @date S1, 2017
11 * @brief The frame buffer allows for higher level post processing effects.
12 *
13 * The frame buffer takes the output rendered to the window and creates a texture (texture buffer) for the data
14 * such as normals and depth information (known as rendering to a texture / off screen rendering).
15 * By creating texture buffers to store data for use in the shaders we can create higher level effects
16 * that are applied to the screen output to a two dimensional texture with three dimensional data (depth, normals etc).
17 * See:
18 * https://learnopengl.com/#!Advanced-OpenGL/Framebuffers
19 * http://www.opengl-tutorial.org/intermediate-tutorials/tutorial-14-render-to-texture/
20 */
